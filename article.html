Greatest XI of All Time

Ronaldo Nazário, also known simply as R9, is one of the most electrifying players football has ever seen. With a blend of explosive pace, ridiculous skill, and clinical finishing, he changed how strikers played the game. A two-time Ballon d'Or winner and World Cup champion, Ronaldo dominated defenders with ease and left fans in awe throughout his career. Many believe that if injuries hadn’t hampered him, he could have set untouchable records. When R9 speaks football, the world listens — and here is his selection of the greatest XI of all time.

Goalkeeper
Gianluigi Buffon
A symbol of consistency and leadership, Buffon is widely regarded as one of the greatest goalkeepers in football history. His reflexes, shot-stopping ability, and longevity at the top level are unmatched.
Right Back
Cafu
The only player to appear in three consecutive World Cup finals, Cafu combined elite stamina, speed, and attacking flair. A true modern fullback before his time, he was a leader on and off the pitch.
Center Back
Franz Beckenbauer
Known as “Der Kaiser,” Beckenbauer revolutionized the role of a sweeper with his calmness, elegance, and passing range. A two-time Ballon d'Or winner and a World Cup champion both as a player and manager.
Center Back
Paolo Maldini
A model of defensive perfection, Maldini was known for his intelligence, timing, and loyalty to AC Milan. He spent over two decades at the top, facing and neutralizing the best attackers in history.
Left Back
Roberto Carlos
With a rocket-powered left foot and incredible pace, Roberto Carlos redefined the left-back role. His famous free kicks and overlapping runs made him a constant attacking threat.
Central Midfield
Zinedine Zidane
A magician with the ball, Zidane's grace, vision, and ability to control the tempo of a match were unparalleled. His performances in major finals — especially the 1998 World Cup and 2002 Champions League — are legendary.
Attacking Midfield
Johan Cruyff
The architect of “Total Football,” Cruyff was as much a thinker as he was a player. His intelligence, dribbling, and influence on the game left a permanent legacy both on and off the pitch.
Attacking Midfield
Diego Maradona
One of the most gifted players ever, Maradona's dribbling, creativity, and flair were out of this world. He single-handedly led Argentina to World Cup glory in 1986 with moments of pure genius.
Right Wing
Lionel Messi
Arguably the greatest of all time, Messi's vision, dribbling, and goal-scoring ability are second to none. With countless individual awards and team trophies, he has consistently performed at an elite level for over 15 years.
Left Wing
Cristiano Ronaldo
A physical specimen and goal machine, Cristiano Ronaldo's work ethic, consistency, and clutch performances have made him one of football's greatest icons. He is a five-time Ballon d’Or winner with success in multiple leagues.
Center Forward
Pelé
A global football pioneer and three-time World Cup winner, Pelé was known for his incredible technique, vision, and goal-scoring ability. He remains a symbol of football greatness and Brazilian excellence.

R9’s greatest XI isn't just a list of legends — it’s a tribute to the brilliance, passion, and evolution of football through the decades. From defensive generals to magical playmakers and unstoppable forwards, this lineup represents the highest standard of the beautiful game.
